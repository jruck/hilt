# Product Scouting Playbook

## Media

![Newsletter reference](/demo/library-newsletter.svg)

## Summary

The playbook argues that product teams should treat meetings, support threads, bookmarks, and research links as one connected signal stream rather than separate inboxes.

## Key Points

- Look for repeated friction across different source types.
- Promote references only when they can change a project decision.
- Keep temporary candidates visible but cheap to discard.
- Feed views should explain why an item is being surfaced.

Strong product discovery systems combine explicit saves with watched sources. Bookmarks and direct sends are high-intent durable signals. Subscriptions and newsletters are lower-intent discovery streams. The distinction lets the system stay aggressive about reading broadly without forcing every artifact into the permanent library.
