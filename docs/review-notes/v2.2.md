# v2.2 — The judge layer

**What changed:** the reweave now renders a direct verdict alongside the weave: an
`attention_judgment` in frontmatter — `tier: high | medium | low` plus a one-line reason — answering
"how much does this deserve Justin's limited attention right now?" The agent judges the *source's*
value to the practice, not the digest's quality, and is told most saves are honestly "low."

**What did NOT change:** the digest voice, connection discipline, and render are byte-identical to
v2.1. Items at v2/v2.1 are not version-behind and will not be re-reweaved.

**What to review:** does the tier match your instinct, and is the reason line specific to *your* work
(names a project, a strand, a real use) rather than generic praise? A judgment that says "high —
interesting article about AI" is a failure; "high — directly tests the de-SaaS thesis you're writing
for the consultancy" is the bar.

**Why:** this is the Library v2 judge layer (`docs/plans/library-v2.md`): the judgment calibrates the
arithmetic worth score (judge–score agreement metric) and feeds For You precision. Authority order:
your verdicts > the judge > the formula.
