# Concision & density

A **partial v1.4 set** — 7 of the 12 regenerated before we stopped; the other 5 stay on v1.3 for now. The job is to check the *shape*: does it read like a brief, not an essay?

**The test cases in this set:**

- **AI News (newsletter)** — the worst v1.3 offender (≈10 heading-then-paragraph sections). Does it now synthesize a couple of threads instead of mirroring the whole issue?
- **Dax Raad / Centaurs** — were prose-heavy and a bit floral. Tighter now? Bullets/tables where they earn it?
- **Karpathy** — was already perfect. Make sure the density push didn't *over*-trim a good one.
- **Jasper (product)** — the broken hero is gone (Interior Define blocked the og:image fetch, so it's no image rather than a broken one); the digest is just the specs.

**What changed in v1.4:** executive-brief density (lead with the takeaway; bullets/tables over prose walls; no floral runway); newsletters synthesized, not mirrored; connections lean first-party, library ties rare (0-1) and deduped against the body.

**Kept on purpose:** the v1.3 voice rules, and calling out when a piece really matters to your work (the "field manual for your North Star" framing on Thariq).

**Flag:** anything still a wall of prose, any newsletter still reading like a table of contents, padded/duplicate connections — and the opposite: anything over-trimmed that lost real substance.
